Menu Structure


Categories

  • Categories are the first thing a customer will see on your ordering terminal.

  • Typically, these are general areas of a menu, for instance “sandwiches” or “lunch menu” could be names for a Category.

  • Categories may contain menu items and/or additional sub-categories

Sub Categories

  • Once your customer picks a category, they can optionally be presented with sub-categories, which will contain recipe items.

  • For example, the Category “Sandwiches” might contain the following Sub-Categories:
    • Burgers
    • Fried Chicken Sandwiches
    • Cold Sandwiches

Sets

  • Sets are the last thing your customer will see once they have chosen a recipe item to order.

  • Sets allow the customer to add free or up-charged items to an individual recipe item.

  • Examples of Sets could be:
    • Dipping Cups
    • Sandwich Toppings
    • Upgrades such as adding sides to an order
